The Present Tense

The Present Tense/ El presente/ El Presente del Indicativo = This tense is used to talk about habitual actions, routine and activities happening now or in the very near future. Example: I eat/ Yo como.

The Past Tense 

The Preterite Tense/ El Préterito = This tense is used to talk about actions completed in the past. Example: I went to the park/ Fui al parque.

The Imperfect Tense/ El imperfecto= This tense is used to describe habitual actions in the past. Example: I used to go swimming every week/ Solía ir a nadar todas las semanas.

The Future Tense 

The Simple Future/ El Futuro Simple = This tense is used to talk about things that will or shall happen. Example: I will go to the beach/ Iré a la playa.

The Informal Future/ El Futuro Informal= The informal future uses a combination of a conjugated form of ir in the present indicative, the preposition a, and an infinitive. It is used to express events in the immediate future and for set plans. Example: I am going to prepare dinner/ Voy a preparar la cena.

The Present Tense

Le Présent= Similar to Spanish and the English simple present, the French present tense is used to talk about facts, current situations and repeated actions in the present. Example: The boy is called Mark/Le garçon s’appelle Mark.

The Past Tense 

Le Passé Composé = This tense is used much like the English simple past. The passé composé talks about actions that were completed in the past and emphasises their results or consequences in the present. We form the passé composé using the auxiliary verbs avoir or être followed by the past participle (le participe passé) of the verb. Example: He went to work/ Il est allé travailler.

L'imparfait= It describes states and actions that were ongoing or repeated in the past. 'Used to' would be a trigger for this tense. Example: When she was younger, she loved swimming/ Quand elle était plus jeune, elle aimait nager.

The Future Tense 

Le futur proche/Le futur composé= Used to talk about actions in the near future. It corresponds to the English structure going 'to + infinitive'. Example: I am going to the park/ Je vais au parc.

Le futur simple= Corresponds to the will-future tense in English. We mostly use this tense to talk about future plans or intentions. Example: I will go to work tomorrow/J'irai travailler demain.
