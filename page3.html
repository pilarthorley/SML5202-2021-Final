The imperfect tense/ El imperfecto

Spanish Imperfect Tense - Regular Verb Endings

-er and -ir verbs have the same endings. Learn the table below:

Personal Pronoun AR endings ER and IR endings
I/Yo -aba -ía
You/Tú -abas -ías
He,She,it/él, ella, usted -aba -ía
We/Nosotros -ábamos -íamos
You (plural)/Vosotros -abais -íais
Them/ellos, ellas, ustedes -aban -ían

Spanish Imperfect Tense - Irregular Verb Endings

These are the only three verbs that are irregular in the imperfect tense. Simple!:

ir (to go) ser (to be) ver (to see)

íbamos éramos veíamos

The French Past Tense/ Le Passé Composé

The French Passé Composé

To conjugate the passé composé we use the present tense of avoir or être as an auxiliary verb, followed by the past participle (participe passé) of the main verb:

Personal Pronoun Present of avoir Present of être Example of past participle
I j'ai je suis aime
You tu as tu es parti
He,She,it il/elle/on a il/elle/on est fini
We nous avons nous sommes vendu
You (plural) vous avez vous êtes dormi
Them ils/elles ont ils/elles sont sorti

Forming the past participle

Here are the endings for each regular verb:

ER verbs IR verbs RE verbs
é (aimer – aimé) i (finir – fini) u (vendre - vendu)

Avoir or être?

Most verbs construct the passé composé with avoir, however être is used as the auxiliary verb with the following 14 verbs of motion: naître/mourir, aller/venir, monter/descendre, arriver/partir, entrer/sortir, apparaître, rester, retourner, tomber and their related forms such as: revenir, rentrer, remonter, redescendre, repartir. Example: Je suis arrivé à la gare.
